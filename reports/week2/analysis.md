# Week 2 Analysis

## Learning Points

During the customer review meeting, the team identified several important improvements required for the project.

The customer emphasized the importance of:

* consistent typography across all screens;
* stronger UX documentation and user flow descriptions;
* more natural and premium visual design choices;
* research-based questionnaire methodology;
* proper Telegram Mini App integration.

The team also learned that aesthetic consistency and usability are equally important for the customer.

---

## Validated Assumptions

Several initial assumptions were confirmed during the meeting:

* Users may not be familiar with fragrance terminology.
* A guided questionnaire approach is appropriate for the target audience.
* Natural color palettes such as olive, beige, and wood-inspired tones are suitable for the product concept.
* Telegram Mini Apps are an appropriate platform for the MVP.

The customer also confirmed that the overall project direction and user stories are acceptable.

---

## Needs Clarification

Some areas still require additional clarification and refinement:

* Final recommendation methodology;
* Exact mapping between questionnaire answers and fragrance recommendations;
* Finalized color palette;
* Authentication flow details;
* Final UX flow structure.

The team also needs to further research fragrance recommendation methodologies and scientific sources for questionnaire design.

---

## Impact on MVP v1

The feedback received during the meeting will directly influence the next MVP iteration.

The team plans to:

* redesign the visual palette;
* standardize typography;
* improve questionnaire quality using research-based sources;
* expand user stories with detailed UX flows;
* improve Telegram Mini App behavior to remain fully inside Telegram.

These improvements are expected to increase usability, visual consistency, and recommendation quality in MVP v1.
