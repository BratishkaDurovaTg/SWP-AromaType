# Customer Meeting Transcript

## Meeting Information
**Date:** June 12, 2026
**Purpose:** Assignment 2 customer review meeting
**Format:** Online call
**Participants:** Product Team Representatives, Customer

---

Interviewer: We have provided you with the user stories. Please take a look and let us know if you agree with all of them.

Customer: Yes, all of them are acceptable.

Interviewer: Here is the link to the prototype in Figma.

Customer: What methodology did you incorporate into the prototype?

Interviewer: This is just an example. The methodology will be provided separately.

Customer: So at this point, we are only reviewing the design, correct?

Interviewer: Yes, that is correct.

Customer: The design looks somewhat cheap and outdated. The concept with dominant brown and green colors is not bad, but I would prefer deeper and richer colors - something like olive, wood tones, and beige. Currently, it looks artificial and synthetic.

Interviewer: Understood. We will take this into account.

Customer: Let's go through the typography. You are using different font types. Different font families should not be used on the same frame under any circumstances. For example, on the first page ("Tell us a little about yourself"), you have three different fonts in one image. That is not acceptable. You can change font sizes, but the typeface should remain consistent. The only possible exception is branding - if your branding uses a specific font, it should still match the rest of the page, including headings and body text.

Interviewer: Understood.

Customer: Also, there are logical errors: it says "describe in three words," but the options provided contain only two words.

Interviewer: Understood. We will address that.

Customer: Overall, the design is fine.

Interviewer: Great. Could you please take a look at the loading screen and the error pages? Aside from color saturation, do you have any other feedback?

Customer: The bottle icon itself does not need to be changed. However, there are again different fonts - please do not use a calligraphic font.

Interviewer: Understood. Do you prefer a more straightforward, minimalist font?

Customer: I would prefer a rounded, minimalist font. For example, Montserrat would work well here, or something similar. The font currently used in the questionnaire is generally fine - I like it. If I were to choose from the fonts shown on the first frame: 17, 21, 24, and 22 are acceptable. Font 29 is interesting but somewhat difficult to read.

Interviewer: Understood. Do you have any other thoughts or notes regarding the design?

Customer: Yes. Green text on a green background is unreadable. Gray text on a green background would work much better. However, since you will be adjusting the colors anyway, this issue may be resolved naturally because the current shades of green differ significantly.

Interviewer: So you would like us to prepare several separate color palettes and send them to you for approval?

Customer: Yes, that would work. Also, it is very important that the solution is well thought out not only in terms of design but also in terms of UX. Please provide more detailed user flows. For example: user clicks a button and is redirected to a specific screen. The user stories should be more specific because currently they are too general. For instance, on the homepage, the user can click "Log in with email" or "Log in with Yandex Mail." Every button should have a documented outcome. This will make the design process much easier and reduce rework later.

Interviewer: Yes, of course. I will pass this feedback to our designer.

Customer: Also, a good accent color for an olive-based palette is pink. I would suggest making olive the primary color, brown or wood tones secondary, and a soft pastel pink for accents. That combination would work very well.

Interviewer: Do you mean that buttons or answer options should be pink?

Customer: No, text should not be pink. Small interface elements could use pink accents. For example, on the "Quest 1" frame, some visible lines could be pink. That would create a pleasant visual accent. However, you need to ensure that the colors work well together and avoid visual overload on each screen. Alternatively, instead of pink, you could use a light pastel gray.

Interviewer: Understood. Is it acceptable to change the main colors as well?

Customer: Yes, absolutely.

Interviewer: Great. Then let's move on to the questionnaire. When developing the methodology, we assumed that users are inexperienced in selecting perfumes. Therefore, we asked broader guiding questions instead of direct questions such as "Do you prefer sweet or sour scents?" because users may not understand fragrance terminology. Instead, we focused on questions unrelated to fragrance notes or specific smells.

Customer: What sources or data did you use to develop these questions?

Interviewer: We developed them independently.

Customer: You should base your questions on sources such as scientific publications or articles related to perfume selection. The questions need to lead to a clear outcome. The questionnaire requires a more substantiated and scientific approach.

Interviewer: Understood. We will definitely improve that. The last thing we would like to show you is the working version of the Telegram Mini App. For now, it is mostly empty.

Customer: Alright. However, I do not like that when I open the Mini App, I am redirected to a browser. After opening the Mini App inside Telegram, I should remain within Telegram.

Interviewer: Understood. That concludes everything we needed to show you for Assignment 2. Thank you for your time.

Customer: Thank you. Goodbye.
