# Week 4 Reflection

## Learning points

During this Sprint, the team learned that customer feedback is most valuable when it is collected through a structured Sprint Review and User Acceptance Testing. The meeting helped identify usability issues that were not obvious during development, especially in the questionnaire and checkout flow.

We also learned the importance of defining clear quality requirements and keeping the Definition of Done up to date. Preparing CI checks, automated testing, and quality requirements helps ensure that new features can be verified consistently before they are considered complete.

Releasing the Sprint increment and discussing it directly with the customer provided useful validation of the implemented functionality and helped prioritize future improvements.

## Validated assumptions

The Sprint Review confirmed that the redesigned questionnaire and recommendation workflow were improvements over the previous version. The customer approved the overall project direction and the updated user interface.

At the same time, some assumptions were revised. The customer preferred a simpler checkout process, recommended changing the order of questionnaire answers, and suggested postponing payment integration until a later Sprint. The need for administrator tools and a real product catalog was also confirmed.

## Friction and gaps

Several improvements identified during UAT still need to be implemented, including questionnaire refinements, checkout improvements, and additional administrator functionality.

Some quality-related work, such as expanding automated tests and increasing test coverage, should continue in future Sprints as the project grows. Additional work is also required to complete the product catalog and improve the overall user experience.

## Planned response

In the next Sprint, we will implement the customer feedback collected during the Sprint Review, continue refining the questionnaire and recommendation workflow, improve the checkout process, and expand administrator functionality.

We will also continue improving automated testing, CI checks, and quality verification while keeping the Product Backlog, Sprint documentation, and Definition of Done up to date.
