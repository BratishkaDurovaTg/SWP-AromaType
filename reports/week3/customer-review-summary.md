# Customer Review Summary

## Overall Feedback

The customer responded positively to the recent design updates and acknowledged noticeable improvements compared to previous iterations. However, they identified two major areas that still require work:

1. Questionnaire methodology and credibility
2. Questionnaire UI and visual design

---

## Questionnaire Feedback

### Main Concern: Lack of Research-Based Methodology

The customer questioned how the questionnaire was created and whether it was based on any research, studies, or established methodology.

Current approach:

* Questions were created based primarily on internal assumptions and product intuition.
* No supporting research or external sources were referenced.

Customer recommendation:

* Find relevant research sources related to fragrance preferences, consumer behavior, personality traits, and scent selection.
* Use AI tools to summarize findings.
* Rebuild or validate questionnaire questions using those sources.
* Include references in documentation.

Expected outcome:

* Increased credibility.
* Easier stakeholder approval.
* Fewer objections regarding question validity.

### Question Flow Concerns

The customer highlighted that question order in the questionnaire is important.

Example issue:

* Asking about allergies at the beginning creates a mismatch between user expectations and questionnaire flow.

Customer expectation:

* Users expect to immediately start discovering fragrance preferences.
* Practical questions such as allergies should appear later or be integrated more naturally.

Recommendation:

* Review question sequencing.
* Consider branching logic or decision-tree-based flows.

## Design Feedback

### Overall Impression

Customer reaction:

> "It looks much more interesting now."

Most positively received areas:

* Recommendation page
* Fragrance profile page
* Product card redesign

---

## Recommendation Page

### Positive Feedback

Strengths:

* Logical structure
* Clean information hierarchy
* Recommendation cards feel organized and easy to read
* Not visually overloaded

### Open Question

Customer questioned whether fragrance category segmentation is necessary.

Example:

* Woody
* Floral
* Green

Concern:

* If only five fragrances are recommended, categorization may add unnecessary complexity.

Recommendation:

* Reevaluate the value of category grouping for small recommendation sets.

---

## Product Page

### Positive Feedback

Customer particularly liked:

* Accord visualization
* Fragrance profile presentation
* Visual representation of fragrance characteristics

Reason:

* Easier to understand than text-heavy descriptions.
* More visually engaging.

---

## Questionnaire UI

### Main Issue: Visual Overload

Customer described the questionnaire as:

> "Too overloaded."

Elements contributing to overload:

* Too many colors
* Large fragrance illustrations
* Step indicators
* Multiple visual accents
* Background gradients/blobs
* Heavy shadows
* Large buttons

Overall effect:

* Visually noisy
* Less premium
* Harder to focus on content

---

## Design Direction Recommended by Customer

### Preferred Style

Customer repeatedly emphasized:

> "Minimalist but premium."

Reference style:

* Similar to an "Old Money" aesthetic translated into product design.

Characteristics:

* Simpler layouts
* Fewer visual effects
* Strong typography hierarchy
* Controlled color usage
* Reduced visual noise

---

## Color Palette Feedback

Positive feedback:

* Existing color palette is considered strong.
* No need to replace it.

Recommendation:

* Keep current palette.
* Improve color distribution and hierarchy.
* Avoid unnecessary color effects.

---

## Shadows and Effects

Customer feedback:

* Heavy shadows and gradients should be reduced.
* Subtle shadows are acceptable when used intentionally.

Guideline:

* Use shadows only to support hierarchy and focus.
* Avoid decorative shadows.

---

## Icon Feedback

Positive feedback:

* Fragrance icons were well received.
* Icons fit the product concept.

Suggested improvement:

* Build a complete icon library in a consistent style.

Minor concern:

* Some icons appear slightly pixelated.

Priority:

* Low.

---

## Action Items

### High Priority

* Add research-backed methodology to the questionnaire.
* Collect and reference external sources.
* Revisit questionnaire structure and question order.
* Explore branching questionnaire logic.

### Medium Priority

* Simplify questionnaire UI.
* Reduce visual clutter.
* Remove unnecessary gradients and shadows.
* Improve color hierarchy.

### Low Priority

* Review fragrance category segmentation in recommendations.
* Create a consistent icon system.
* Improve icon quality where possible.

---

## Customer Sentiment

Overall sentiment: Positive

Customer acknowledged clear progress in the product and design direction. The primary concern is not functionality but credibility of the questionnaire methodology and reducing visual complexity in the questionnaire experience.
